
# Latent Behavioral Structure and Clustering Analysis of Gen Z Wellbeing Using Exploratory Factor Analysis

## 📌 Project Overview

This project explores the relationship between digital lifestyle behaviors and psychological wellbeing using a multivariate statistical approach. Rather than relying on simple correlation analysis, the study applies Exploratory Factor Analysis (EFA) to uncover latent constructs underlying digital exposure and mental health indicators. The extracted factor scores are then used in K-Means clustering to identify distinct behavioral segments.

The objective is to understand whether high digital engagement consistently leads to psychological distress and to identify structured wellbeing risk profiles within the dataset.

---

## 🗂 Dataset Description

* 10,000 synthetic observations
* Age range: 18–26
* Variables include:

  * Screen Time Hours
  * Daily Social Media Hours
  * Night Scrolling Frequency
  * Anxiety Score
  * Emotional Fatigue Score
  * Sleep Quality Score
  * Social Comparison Index
  * Wellbeing Index

---

## 🧠 Methodology

### 1️. Data Preparation

* Data cleaning and scaling
* Standardization before factor analysis

### 2️. Exploratory Factor Analysis (EFA)

* KMO & Bartlett’s Test for sampling adequacy
* Eigenvalue-based factor extraction
* Identification of latent constructs:

  * **Factor 1: Digital Engagement Intensity**
  * **Factor 2: Psychological Distress**

### 3️. Clustering

* K-Means clustering using factor scores
* Optimal cluster selection via Elbow Method
* 3 distinct behavioral segments identified

---

## 📈 Key Findings

* Digital engagement and psychological distress form separate latent dimensions.
* High screen time does not automatically imply high psychological distress.
* Three distinct behavioral profiles emerge:

  * High Digital – Stable
  * Low Digital – Low Distress
  * Psychologically Vulnerable

The results highlight that digital wellbeing is multidimensional and structurally segmented.
